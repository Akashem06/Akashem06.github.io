Battery management firmware for the Midnight Sun solar car's main traction pack: 288 lithium-ion cells arranged in an 8P36S configuration, rated at 150V and 52A.

The front-end measurement is handled by LTC6811 battery monitor ICs accessed over isolated SPI. The firmware polls cell voltages, runs passive balancing when any cell is above the target, monitors 16+ thermistors for thermal faults, and feeds all of that into a CAN-based telemetry stream for remote diagnostics.

The state-of-charge estimator is the most technically involved part. A pure Coulomb counter drifts over time, and open-circuit voltage lookup is too slow to use under dynamic loads. The solution is a 1-RC equivalent circuit battery model running an extended Kalman filter, fusing Coulomb counting and voltage mapping in real time. The model was developed and validated in MATLAB and Simulink, then autogenerated into embedded C for deployment on the STM32. Final accuracy on the test rig was 78% across a full charge and discharge cycle.
